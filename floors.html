Classroom Style

The classroom style is a training room layout that mimics the look and feel of a traditional classroom setting. Training tables are lined up in rows facing the front of the room where the instructor stands.

U-Shape style

U-shape setup will help facilitate discussion with participants facing each other, but with one end up, a U-shape setup is ideal for seminars and group meetings with presentations.

Theater style

This is a type of setup in which the tables and chairs are arranged in rows and they all face toward one side mostly the podium.

Hollow square style

Conference style

Large rectangular tables or oval tables with chairs around.

Boardroom style

Used for formal meeting, the standard is using two Oblong tables and use two half-moon tables at the edge.

Lecture theatre training rooms

This type of arrangement is used when addressing a large number of learners.

Herringbone style

Tables and chairs are angled for better line of vision and engagement between participants themselves as well as with the speaker.
